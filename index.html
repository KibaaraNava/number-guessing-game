The Lord of The Rings by J.R.R. Tolkien Genre: Fantasy, High Fantasy

The Lord of the Rings is a seminal work of high fantasy that features a richly developed world, Middle-earth. It follows the journey of a group of heroes attempting to destroy the One Ring to prevent the dark lord Sauron from taking over the world. The series includes themes of friendship, good versus evil, and personal sacrifice.

1984 by George Orwell Genre: Dystopian Fiction, Political Fiction, Science Fiction

1984 is a classic work of dystopian fiction that explores themes of totalitarianism, surveillance, and oppression. It is set in a future society where the government, led by "Big Brother," controls every aspect of citizens' lives. The novel critiques political systems and serves as a warning about the dangers of unchecked power.

The Metamorphosis by Franz Kafka Genre: Surrealist Fiction, Existential Fiction

TThe Metamorphosis is a short novella that explores themes of alienation, identity, and absurdity through the story of Gregor Samsa, who wakes up to find himself transformed into a giant insect. It is considered one of the most important works in the genre of existential and absurdist fiction, often examining the disconnect between individuals and society.

The Batman (comic and film adaptations) Genre: Superhero Fiction, Action, Crime Fiction

The Batman is a superhero genre story that follows Bruce Wayne as he assumes the alter ego of Batman to fight crime and corruption in Gotham City. The character is often associated with crime noir, as Batman's dark, brooding nature and his battle against Gotham's criminal underworld highlight themes of justice, morality, and vengeance.
